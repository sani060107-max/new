Hajj Guidelines

A complete guide to performing Hajj over 5 days and its benefits.

Day 1: Arrival & Ihram

Pilgrims enter the state of Ihram at the Miqat, perform Niyyah (intention), and recite the prescribed duas. Travel to Mina and settle for the night. Pilgrims begin their sacred journey by donning the Ihram and moving to Mina, a valley that becomes alive with tents and prayers. Here, they prepare themselves spiritually with continuous Talbiyah, heartfelt supplications, and reflection. The atmosphere is filled with brotherhood and equality, reminding pilgrims that this is a journey of unity and humility in front of Allah.

Day 2: Arafat

Travel to Arafat, spend the afternoon in supplication and prayer. This is the most important day of Hajj. The Day of Arafat is the heart of Hajj. Pilgrims gather on the plains of Arafat, standing in sincere devotion under the open sky. Here, they listen to the Khutbah and spend their time in Dua, seeking forgiveness and mercy. It is said that no day holds more blessings, and every pilgrim feels the closeness of Allah as they pour out their hopes and sorrows.

Day 3: Muzdalifah & Eid Sacrifice

Move to Muzdalifah after sunset at Arafat, collect pebbles for Rami (stoning), perform prayers, and return to Mina for the Eid sacrifice. The overnight stay is a significant stage of the pilgrimage, representing humility and preparing for the final rituals.Pilgrims spend the night under the open sky in Muzdalifah, collecting pebbles for the symbolic stoning of the devil. This simple act strengthens their resolve against temptation. At dawn, they return to Mina, where they perform the stoning of Jamarat, a powerful reminder of Prophet Ibrahim‚Äôs steadfastness. Sacrifice (Qurbani) is also offered, symbolizing obedience and gratitude to Allah.

Surrounded by millions of believers, each pilgrim feels a deep connection to the Ummah, gaining patience, humility, and inner peace.

Day 5: Final Rituals & Tawaf

Return to Mecca, perform Tawaf Al-Wada (farewell circumambulation) around the Ka'bah, completing the Hajj pilgrimage.The final day of Hajj is a bittersweet moment of farewell. Pilgrims perform the Tawaf al-Wida around the Kaaba, bidding goodbye to the sacred sanctuary. Each step is filled with gratitude, reflection, and a prayer for acceptance of their Hajj. With hearts purified and souls renewed, pilgrims leave Makkah carrying memories of devotion, unity, and an everlasting bond with Allah.

Benefits of Performing Hajj

  • Forgiveness of sins and spiritual purification
  • Strengthening faith and closeness to Allah
  • Unity and equality among Muslims worldwide
  • Personal reflection and renewal of intentions
  • Opportunity for duas and seeking guidance
